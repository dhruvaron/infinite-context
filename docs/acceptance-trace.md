### Intentional web-evidence boundary

The former GAP-MEM-05 wording assumed that a provider citation span was equivalent to locally fetched page text. It is not. V1 persists the answer, citation title/URL/span, timestamps, freshness class, and provenance, but does not claim possession of the cited page body unless separate exact source bytes were actually ingested. The compiler may attribute the answer to the citation metadata; it must not create exact page-text claims from unseen content. `apps/api/src/orchestrator-web.test.ts` locks this conservative behavior.

## Release decision

The repository may be demonstrated as schema-18 prototype source with current commit-bound deterministic 10,000-message and 100,000-event local artifacts, alongside separately labeled historical/ineligible causal diagnostics. Current local, browser, load, and no-cost gates materially improve implementation confidence, but the revision may not be called release-validated while the deterministic 15% gate is missed and fresh-checkout, paid/live, competitor, latency, and dense-scale evidence remain incomplete. It may not be described as empirically superior to ChatGPT, Codex, rolling compaction, or flat retrieval, and it may not claim live latency targets, full-compiler O(delta) behavior, or proven scalability to thousands of genuine durable facts. Recorded provider spend is USD 0.00. The verdict remains **NOT ACCEPTED** until external evidence and scale blockers are closed or explicitly waived in the binding plan.
